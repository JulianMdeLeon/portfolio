Senior Project: Detection of Manufacturing Defects in Ceramic Matrix Composites via Machining Force Signatures

Full Report

  • Designed and constructed a testing fixture to detect and measure the forces applied by a vertical mill to a sample of ceramic matrix composites using Fusion360 and a three axis mill, band saw, and lathe.

  • Operated the vertical mill and testing fixture to conduct tests on Ceramic Matrix Composites to acquire data which proved that defects in Ceramic Matrix Composites can be detected during machining.

  • Cooperated with engineers of Lockheed Martin to iterate and improve the accuracy and reliability of the testing apparatus.

  • Modifications we would have liked to make if permitted more time for development:

    • Add inlet holes to the acrylic dust box to reduce turbulence inside. Current design only has the top hole for the drill and the side hole for the vacuum. This caused the vacuum to intake air from the drill opening. If smaller air inlet holes were added to the side opposite of the vacuum hole, this should streamline the airflow for dust collection.

    • Longer wiring to move the computer further from the mill.

    • Redesign center mounting plate for small test specimens to maintain simple calculations via only drilling in the center.
